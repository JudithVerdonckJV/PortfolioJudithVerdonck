Modelling and texturing

All the 3D projects I have made were all for assignments at DAE. The very first one was the cityscene, for which I chose to model a Tarifa-inspired part. For a game dev, I definitely spend too much time on this, having fun with adding detail and life to the scene.
In general, we were restricted to low poly, and texturing was done with images in photoshop. There was no hard restriction on material slots used, but they did guide us to only use diffuse and opacity. Apart from that, I tried to keep it as diverse as possible while also reusing things like windows and plants.

The second project I made (and again spend too much time on because I was having fun with it) was a high poly model, which we then baked down onto a low poly version. We were free to choose what we used as reference, as long as it was made of at least two different (PBR) materials and had at least two hierarchical joints. In the end, I chose a robotic animal leg, which looked very organic while still having mechanical elements in it.
We were never shown how to sculpt, so this leg is completely made with simple modeling. We used Substance Painter for texturing, having a bit of an introduction to it and then basically let loose on it. I mixed some smart generators with manual painting for the end result.
