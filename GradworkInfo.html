Forest Fire simulator

Graduation work

The project

As part of our education at Digital Arts and Entertainment, we all do a graduation work involving research, making a project based on that research, and writing a paper about the whole process. I chose the topic "forest fire simulation", which was in cooperation with the University of Leuven. I chose to follow the needs and desires from KU Leuven as closely as possible, although this was not required; they could give topics, but were not clients.

While the topic was indeed wildfire simulation, the actual goal was to visualize a simplified version of the electric signals going over the heart. As such, I made it my primary goal to make a system that would allow me to correctly show wave propagation on a 3D model.

Research

I started my research very broad, looking at recent wildfires and gathering some general information. After that, I started looking into the workings of a wildfire; what causes them to gain or loose speed, what kind of wildfires are there etc. Of course, since I needed to combine this with the human heart, I also briefly looked into the workings of the heart. With the resources provided by KU Leuven, I was able to filter out what I would need for this project.

Simulator

Grid

The first step was to generate a grid large enough to encapsulate the entire mesh, but small enough to not cause unnessecary calculation. This also meant it would need to be hollow - the inside of a mesh should not have any wave propagation going on.
The spawning of the grids was made so the user can choose how many cells there are.

Transform: trees and fire

Another difficulty was the transform the trees and fire VFX should have. "Up" wasn't positive Z anymore, and taking the center of a cell did not guarantee it would be anywhere near the terrain surface. I ended up doing linetraces for every edge of the cell and taking an average from all the impact normals and positions. This worked decently well for most geometry and was fast enough to be useble for grid generation.

Paper

We needed to write a paper documenting our research and the making of the project . I also ran tests, looking at fps in various scenarios, and discussed the results.
